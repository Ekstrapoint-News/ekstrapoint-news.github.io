## What Is a Stock Split?

A stock split happens when a company divides its existing shares into multiple new shares. For example, in a 2-for-1 split, each shareholder gets an extra share for every one they already own, and the price per share is halved. The total value of your holdings stays the same—think of it like slicing a pizza into more pieces; you still have the whole pizza!

**Why do companies do this?**  
Usually, it’s to make their stock more affordable and attractive to a broader range of investors. Lower share prices can bring in new buyers, boost trading activity, and often create a surge of fresh excitement.
